Titan MMS — Enterprise Maintenance Management Software Built by Logic Unit
When an organization loses equipment to an unplanned failure, the cost is never just the repair bill. There is lost production, a scramble to reassign technicians, frustrated customers, and a compliance log that now has a gap nobody can explain. Multiply that by hundreds of assets across multiple facilities and the problem becomes a serious drag on profitability.
That is the problem Logic Unit set out to solve when it began designing Titan MMS — a cloud-based Maintenance Management Software (CMMS) and Asset Management platform built for organizations that can no longer afford to run their operations on spreadsheets, paper-based work orders, and disconnected maintenance tools.
Drawing on 15+ years of enterprise software engineering experience, Logic Unit built Titan MMS from the ground up: purpose-designed for real industrial workflows, scalable to enterprise demands, and accessible from any device in the field. Today, Titan MMS serves organizations across manufacturing, healthcare, hospitality, construction, warehousing, and more — giving maintenance teams the visibility, control, and automation they need to operate at peak efficiency.

The Industry Challenges That Demanded a Better Solution
Before designing a single module of Titan MMS, the Logic Unit product team spent extensive time researching the pain points that maintenance managers, facility directors, and operations leaders deal with every day. The findings painted a consistent picture across industries.
Unplanned equipment downtime costing organizations thousands of dollars per hour in lost production
Poor asset visibility — no single source of truth for asset condition, location, or maintenance history
Manual, paper-based maintenance tracking that created compliance gaps and audit failures
Missed preventive maintenance schedules leading to accelerated equipment wear and shorter asset lifespans
Inefficient work order management with no real-time tracking of technician assignments or job status
Spare parts shortages discovered only when a breakdown occurred — not before
High maintenance costs driven by reactive, break-fix approaches rather than planned strategies
Compliance and safety audit failures caused by incomplete or inaccessible maintenance records
Technician coordination problems in multi-site or multi-shift operations
Data silos and reporting blind spots preventing management from making data-driven decisions

These challenges do not exist in isolation. A missed preventive maintenance task leads to an unplanned breakdown. An unplanned breakdown reveals a spare parts shortage. The shortage delays the repair. The delay creates a compliance record gap. Each problem compounds the next — and every one of them has a measurable impact on productivity, operational efficiency, and the bottom line.

The Market Gap Logic Unit Identified
Despite the clear operational cost of poor maintenance management, a large number of mid-market and enterprise organizations were still running their maintenance operations on tools that were never designed for the job. Maintenance teams used Microsoft Excel to track assets, printed paper for work orders, and relied on tribal knowledge to remember PM schedules. Some organizations used legacy systems that required expensive on-premises infrastructure and provided limited mobile capability.
At the same time, the software solutions that did exist were either too complex and expensive for smaller operations to adopt, or too lightweight to handle multi-site enterprise requirements. There was a clear gap in the market for a modern, SaaS-based CMMS platform that combined enterprise-grade capability with practical usability.
That gap became the product vision behind Titan MMS.

Our Solution: How Titan MMS Was Designed and Built
Logic Unit approached the development of Titan MMS the same way it approaches all enterprise software products: with deep domain research, iterative design, and an engineering culture that prioritizes real-world usability alongside technical performance.
The result is a comprehensive CMMS and asset management platform organized around a set of tightly integrated modules, each designed to solve a specific operational challenge.

Asset Management
Every organization needs a single, authoritative record for every asset it owns or operates. Titan MMS provides a complete digital asset register with full lifecycle tracking — from procurement and commissioning through to retirement. Maintenance history, warranty information, depreciation data, and linked documentation are all accessible in one place.
Preventive Maintenance
Titan MMS replaces missed PM schedules with automated, calendar-based and meter-based maintenance triggers. Teams can define maintenance plans at the asset level, set recurrence rules, and let the system handle scheduling automatically — eliminating the risk of human oversight causing a compliance failure or accelerated equipment wear.
Work Order Management
The work order engine is at the core of daily maintenance operations in Titan MMS. Maintenance managers can create, assign, prioritize, and track work orders in real time. Technicians receive job notifications, update job status from the field, and submit completed work with photos and notes attached — creating an auditable record without paperwork.
Equipment Tracking & Downtime Monitoring
Real-time equipment status tracking and downtime logging give operations managers the visibility they need to understand where breakdowns are happening, how long assets are offline, and what the cumulative impact is on production. These insights feed directly into maintenance planning and budget decisions.
Inventory & Spare Parts Management
Titan MMS connects maintenance activities to the spare parts inventory. When a work order is created, technicians can check parts availability before going to the job. Automatic reorder alerts prevent stockouts, and inventory transaction histories keep procurement teams informed.
Facility Management
For organizations managing physical infrastructure — buildings, HVAC systems, electrical installations, and safety equipment — Titan MMS provides dedicated facility management workflows including space management, inspection scheduling, and compliance tracking.
Mobile Maintenance Operations
Titan MMS is fully mobile-accessible. Technicians on the floor, in the field, or across multiple sites can access work orders, update asset records, log time, and capture inspection findings from any smartphone or tablet — with or without constant internet connectivity.
Reporting & Analytics
Management dashboards, KPI tracking, and exportable reports give decision-makers the data they need to evaluate maintenance performance, calculate cost per asset, track mean time between failures (MTBF), and identify opportunities for efficiency improvement.

Technology & Product Engineering Excellence
The technical architecture behind Titan MMS reflects Logic Unit's commitment to building software that performs reliably at scale, adapts to organizational growth, and integrates with the technology stacks that enterprises already use.
SaaS Architecture
Titan MMS is delivered as a true multi-tenant SaaS platform. Organizations access the full feature set through a web browser or mobile app, with no local server installation required. Updates, security patches, and new features are delivered continuously without disruption to operations.

Cloud-Based Deployment
Built on cloud infrastructure, Titan MMS delivers consistent performance for organizations with single-site or multi-country operations. Data is backed up automatically, disaster recovery is built in, and organizations pay only for the capacity they use.

Scalability
Whether an organization manages 50 assets or 50,000, Titan MMS scales to meet the demand. The platform is designed to support enterprise growth without requiring infrastructure investment or re-implementation projects.

Security & Compliance
Titan MMS incorporates role-based access control, audit logging, encrypted data transmission, and data residency options to meet the security and compliance requirements of regulated industries including healthcare and utilities.

Automation Capabilities
Workflow automation in Titan MMS reduces the administrative burden on maintenance teams. Automated work order generation, notification routing, escalation rules, and approval workflows keep operations moving without manual intervention.

Why Logic Unit Built Titan MMS
The decision to develop Titan MMS was not made in a boardroom. It came from years of conversations with operations managers, maintenance supervisors, and facility directors who were struggling with tools that were not designed for their work.
Logic Unit's engineering team heard the same story repeatedly: organizations had invested in ERP systems that handled finance and HR beautifully, but left maintenance management as an afterthought. Technicians were still walking to a printer to collect a paper work order. Maintenance managers were rebuilding asset history from email chains. Compliance auditors were being handed binders of handwritten logs.
The Logic Unit team believed that maintenance management deserved the same level of software craftsmanship that had been applied to CRM, ERP, and financial systems. The engineering challenge was to build a platform that was genuinely useful to a technician in a manufacturing plant at 2am, and simultaneously capable of generating a board-level maintenance cost report for the CFO the next morning.
That design challenge shaped every decision in Titan MMS — from the mobile-first interface to the enterprise-grade reporting engine. It is why the platform continues to evolve based on direct feedback from the maintenance professionals who use it every day.

FAQ Section
1. What is Titan MMS and who developed it?
Titan MMS is a cloud-based Maintenance Management Software (CMMS) and Asset Management platform developed and engineered by Logic Unit. It is designed to help organizations across multiple industries streamline maintenance operations, manage asset lifecycles, reduce downtime, and improve regulatory compliance.
2. What industries does Titan MMS serve?
Titan MMS is built for organizations across manufacturing, healthcare, hospitality, construction, education, warehousing, commercial facilities, utilities, retail, and logistics. The platform's flexible architecture allows it to be configured for the specific operational requirements of each sector.
3. What is the difference between CMMS software and EAM software?
CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System) software focuses on managing maintenance work orders, scheduling, and technician coordination. EAM (Enterprise Asset Management) software extends this to include the full asset lifecycle, including procurement, depreciation, and retirement. Titan MMS combines both capabilities in a single platform.
4. Is Titan MMS available as a cloud-based solution?
Yes. Titan MMS is delivered as a true SaaS platform hosted on cloud infrastructure. Organizations access the platform through a web browser or mobile application, with no local server installation required. Updates and new features are deployed continuously without operational disruption.
5. Can Titan MMS support multiple sites or locations?
Titan MMS is specifically designed for multi-site operations. Organizations can manage assets, maintenance schedules, work orders, and reporting across multiple facilities, warehouses, or geographic locations from a single platform with consolidated and site-level reporting views.
6. How does Titan MMS help with preventive maintenance compliance?
Titan MMS automates PM scheduling based on calendar intervals, meter readings, or operational triggers. When maintenance is performed, technicians complete digital checklists and submit photographic evidence through the mobile app — creating a complete, auditable compliance record that is always accessible for regulatory inspections.
7. What are the integration capabilities of Titan MMS?
Titan MMS is built with an open API architecture that enables integration with ERP platforms, financial systems, procurement tools, IoT sensor networks, and building management systems. The Logic Unit engineering team works directly with enterprise customers to design and implement integration solutions.
8. What future capabilities are planned for Titan MMS?
Logic Unit's product roadmap for Titan MMS includes AI-powered predictive maintenance, IoT asset health monitoring, advanced analytics and benchmarking, expanded enterprise workflow automation, and deeper ERP ecosystem integrations. These capabilities are being developed in direct response to feedback from Titan MMS customers.

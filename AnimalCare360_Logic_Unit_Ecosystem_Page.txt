AnimalCare360: How Logic Unit Built Pakistan's Most Complete Animal Care & Livestock ERP

1. Introduction — A Software Story Born in Pakistan's Fields and Farms
Pakistan is home to one of the world's largest livestock economies. With over 200 million animals across dairy, beef, poultry, and trading sectors, the industry generates hundreds of billions of rupees annually. Yet until recently, most farms, wanda shops, maweshi mandis, and veterinary clinics were running entirely on paper registers, disconnected spreadsheets, and word-of-mouth follow-ups.
Logic Unit, a software engineering company with over 15 years of experience building enterprise-grade SaaS products, saw this gap firsthand. After working across retail, logistics, healthcare, and financial technology, the Logic Unit engineering team turned its attention to an equally critical but chronically underserved sector: Pakistan's animal care and livestock industry.
The result is AnimalCare360 — a cloud-based, FBR-compliant ERP platform engineered from the ground up to serve cattle farms, dairy operations, poultry businesses, wanda feed retailers, animal traders, livestock markets, pet hospitals, and veterinary clinics. Not as a generic software retrofit, but as a purpose-built digital ecosystem that understands how these businesses actually work in Pakistan.

2. Industry Challenges — The Problems Logic Unit Set Out to Solve
Before writing a single line of code, the Logic Unit product team spent months conducting field interviews, visiting farms in Punjab, walking through wanda distribution hubs, and speaking with pet hospital administrators in Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad. Four distinct problem clusters emerged across four business types.
Livestock & Dairy Farm Operations
No structured animal record system — purchase dates, breed history, and pedigree were scribbled in notebooks
Milk yield, weight gain (ADG), and fattening performance tracked manually — or not at all
Vaccination schedules missed due to lack of reminder systems, increasing animal mortality risk
Palai (profit-sharing) partnerships managed through informal verbal agreements, causing revenue disputes
Farm owners had zero real-time financial visibility — no way to know cost-per-animal or lot profitability
Feed Retail & Wanda Shops
Inventory discrepancies due to manual stock registers — ghost stock and silent shortages were common
Expiry management for feed, medicine, and veterinary products handled informally, creating compliance risk
Customer khata (credit) tracked on paper, leading to bad debt and unrecoverable balances
No purchase order system — supplier coordination was reactive and prone to delays
FBR invoicing non-compliance — businesses were vulnerable to tax penalties
Animal Trading Businesses & Maweshi Mandis
Transactions recorded inconsistently or not at all — no per-deal profit tracking
Animal ownership transfer documentation was paper-based and easily lost
Commission, transport cost, and intermediary fees tracked manually, obscuring real margins
Customer relationship management was non-existent — repeat buyers had no system-supported records
Pet Hospitals & Veterinary Clinics
Paper-based patient EMR — pet histories lost between visits or staff changes
Appointment scheduling done verbally or by phone, resulting in no-shows and double bookings
Vaccination and deworming reminders not automated — pet owners missed critical health milestones
Pharmacy inventory managed separately from clinic billing, causing revenue leakage
Administrative overhead consumed significant vet time that should have been spent on patient care

3. Identifying the Market Gap — A Sector with No Fit-for-Purpose Software
The Logic Unit team identified a critical void: the few ERP systems available in Pakistan were either agricultural tools designed for crop farming or generic business software with no understanding of animal lifecycles, livestock trading mechanics, or veterinary workflows. Feed retailers were using grocery POS systems that couldn't track expiry batches or FBR invoicing. Cattle farms were using nothing.
The research also revealed that Pakistan's animal industry is deeply relationship-driven and operationally unique. The concept of palai partnerships, the structure of maweshi mandis, the khata-based credit culture in wanda retail, and the WhatsApp-based communication patterns of farm staff — none of this was addressed by any existing software product.
Logic Unit's conclusion was clear: Pakistan's animal industry needed a platform built specifically for it. Not adapted from somewhere else. Built from scratch, in consultation with the people running these businesses every day.

4. Building AnimalCare360 — Engineering a Four-Module Digital Ecosystem
Logic Unit's engineering team structured AnimalCare360 around four specialized ERP modules, each solving the distinct challenges of a different business type, while sharing a unified data layer, cloud infrastructure, and reporting engine. Here is how each module was built and what it solves.
Module 1: Cattle & Dairy Farm ERP (CattlePro)
CattlePro is the most feature-rich component of AnimalCare360, built across 16 operating modules. Every animal receives a complete digital profile from the moment it enters the farm — whether purchased at a mandi or born on-site.
▸ Animal lifecycle records: purchase, birth, breed, category (A/B/C), pedigree, and tag ID
▸ Daily milk yield tracking with animal-wise trends and buyer supply logs
▸ Weight gain (ADG) and feed conversion ratio (FCR) monitoring for fattening lots
▸ Health and vaccination records with scheduled alerts and withdrawal period tracking
▸ Palai/partnership ROI module for transparent profit-sharing with named partners
▸ Feed and medicine inventory synced directly to animal consumption records
▸ FBR-compliant purchase and sale documentation for full financial audit trails
Module 2: Wanda & Feed Retail ERP
Built for wanda shops, feed distributors, pet food retailers, and veterinary pharmacies, this module turns a traditionally paper-heavy retail operation into a digitally governed business.
▸ High-speed POS with barcode scanning, customer pricing tiers, and cash/credit sales
▸ Multi-warehouse inventory with batch tracking and expiry date management
▸ Customer credit (khata) management with balance alerts and payment history
▸ Supplier purchase orders, cost tracking, and bulk stock arrival management
▸ FBR QR-coded invoices and purchase tax reconciliation built into every transaction
▸ 99.9% inventory accuracy target through real-time stock synchronization
Module 3: Animal Trading & Maweshi Mandi ERP
This module was engineered to handle the transaction complexity of animal trading businesses — from individual deals to large lot sales at livestock markets.
▸ Per-deal recording of purchase, sale, commission, transport, and net profit
▸ Animal lot management with individual ownership transfer documentation
▸ Customer and broker relationship tracking with full transaction history
▸ Pricing consistency controls to prevent undercutting and margin erosion
▸ Real-time deal profitability dashboards visible to owners and managers
Module 4: Pet Hospital & Veterinary Clinic ERP
Built for multi-doctor pet clinics and hospital chains, this module covers the complete patient journey from first appointment to long-term wellness tracking.
▸ Patient EMR: pet profile, owner details, visit history, diagnosis, and treatment plans
▸ Appointment scheduling with multi-room, multi-doctor calendar and WhatsApp reminders
▸ Surgery and lab records with consent forms, procedure notes, and attachments
▸ Pharmacy and dispensary inventory with expiry alerts and FBR-compliant billing
▸ Automated vaccination and follow-up reminders — reducing no-shows and improving outcomes

7. Why Logic Unit Built AnimalCare360 — The Engineering Team's Story
The decision to build AnimalCare360 was not purely commercial. It was rooted in a conviction held by Logic Unit's founders: that technology should serve industries that need it most, not just the industries that already have it.
The Logic Unit team observed that Pakistan's livestock sector contributes significantly to the national GDP, employs tens of millions of people across the rural-to-urban supply chain, and touches nearly every household through dairy products, meat, and animal healthcare. Yet the technology investment in this sector was near zero compared to equivalent industries in developed markets.
Customer interviews conducted during the research phase revealed something important: the pain was not a lack of awareness about technology — farm owners and wanda managers were already using WhatsApp, Android phones, and digital payment platforms. The barrier was that no software existed that understood their business model. They needed software that knew what a palai was. That understood the concept of a wanda. That could handle the way a maweshi mandi actually transacts.
So Logic Unit built exactly that. Customer feedback from early adopters shaped the development roadmap: palai partnership reporting was added after dealer input; WhatsApp-based reminders were built in response to clinic owners who wanted alerts but not email; the Android field app was prioritized after farm managers explained they spend most of their day away from a desk.
